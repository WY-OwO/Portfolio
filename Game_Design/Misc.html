ARTG170 Capstone Game - Go With The Flow

Play the game on itch.io!

For one of my capstone game projects, I joined a team of wonderful artists, writers and programmer to create the game "Go With The Flow". Following the prompt "Collage: 'Verse Jumping", we have decided to make a runner game about our main character, Beanie, chasing after his friend, Flow, through three different dimensions. With each dimension, there's a change in the reason why Beanie chases after Flow. Their relationship develops from playful to protecting, to a happy marriage. Or so it was intended to be. Due to time constraint, the game is unpolished.

I have contributed the character concept of Beanie and Flow, dimension 2's character portraits, both dimensions 1 and 2's animation, and dimension 2's monster design.

ARTG171 Capstone Game - Walk With Us

Game link TBA

I've joined the production team of "Walk With Us" as an external collaborator during my fifth year in UCSC. I have joined as a character concept and background artist. I've also volunteered to take on any miscellaneous art tasks unrelated to what we agreed on, as majority of other available tasks were well within my skill range.

Backgrounds

Referencing google image serach and memories of natural paths in the UCSC campus, I worked on four backgrounds: straight road, curved road, uphill, and downhilll. I was given artistic freedom on how to draw those backgrounds, with the guideline that there must be lost of trees, thick canopy that almost completely covvers the sky, and is about the similar style like their previous placeholder background. Working on those backgrounds was relatively straightforward for me, as someone who had been studying in a forest environment like UCSC.

The straight road background was drafted fist as a chance to check my understanding of the assignment with the team, and thankfully was received well! A fellow artist in the team finalized this draft and I used it as a style guidance for the other three backgrounds. I became more adventurous with what ot include in the curve road background, adding grass and rocks for extra flavor. I've also added a tree stump for variation among the trees, giving the path more layers visually.

For the uphill background, I had a general uphill trail I saw on campus in the back of my head. WIthout any reference I gave the trail a shot and it turned out very well. I added a fallen tree in the background for extra flavor and dimension. While working on the line art, I added some moss and mushrooms on the stump for a natural decaying look. I played around with the shading on the road to make up for the lack of visible treetop leaves.

I struggled with the downhill background the most during this project. I knew the angle will be heavily dependent on the trees around it, but the road was still looking very odd if I draw by pure imagination. After searching up some downhill images on Google and Pinterest, I drastically increased the amount of curves on the trail and reduced the amount of trees but amde them bigger. I intent to have the top of distant trees to be covering a lot of the distant views, since they're lower than the point of view. During coloring and shading, I added more lighting on the top of the canopy in the distance to make it look more apparent it's the top of the tree, not the side.

Character concept: Tea Fairy and Cloaked Jester

I've recently picked up an interest in fae characters and I thought designing a tea fairy based on the limited amount of information provded by the group would be an interesting experience for me to broaden my character design variety.

I started off with a small design in mind, a fae with clothes and accessories made of natural materials like leaves and bark. Bark arm bracers can be a great defense in the forest while foraging. A shawl made of the finest tea leaves they find would be most convenient and practical for these creatures, I reckon. A dress made of flower petals can add variation to what tea they potentially make, ones based on flowers rather than simply leaves. A fanny pack was requested by the team as a mean for carrying other materials.

Since this fairy closely relates to nature, it would make sense for them to have physical natural elements, specifically tea bush themed traits like root legs and bark-textured horns. I left the eyes blank and narrowly shaped to add a sly feeling to the design, since according to the written description, they could treat their guests with the best tea or poison them. I thought a witty look would fit this michievious nature. I shaped its hair to a leafy texture because I think they would want to blend into tea bushes. This design was done on paper first, and finalized digitally.

For the cloaked jester, I was working with only a concept sketch from the ideation of the game. I was given the impression that the character will be wearing a mask and is mischevious, as suggested by the name. I gave it a simple mask with a split face, which was inspired by ENA by Joel G on Youtube, with half a smile and one eye. I thought the phsyique of this character can be more eerie, and I made it taller with a longer neck. The crude cross stitches on the sides was intended to make it look a little scrappy, as it's encountered in the forest. The rest of the body is covered by the long cloak dragging behind.
