3D Modeling

A long process...

I am proficient with Blender, Adobe Substance Painter, and Adobe Substance Designer.

The reference sheet to the right is the finalized concept (at the time of the project) of my character, Cadance. The majority of the following image showcases will be the process of how I modeled him into a rigged 3D character.

Apologies the slideshow buttons are not working well, I'm trying to fix them. JavaScript is not my strong suit.

Body, Hands, Feet

Character is drafted out into A-pose with no coloring for cleaner visuals. Closeup on hands for details and separate modeling.

The reference image is then imported to Blender and the body is built. I pinpointed clothes that will be on the base so there's less to model in the clothes step. That includes the jeans and crop top.

Clothes

The extra clothes on top of the base pose/clothes will be added here. For this character, this includes the jacket and the belt on the back above the tail.

It was at this step that I realize T-pose for modeling character will be more ideal, since I was having trouble with the jacket's sleeve, unable to properly shape the parts of the underarm. In hindsight, weight painting will be more difficult than it already will be.

Head Sculpting and Retopology

Sculpting the head and retopologize on top, maintaining all shapes as quadrilaterals for best effect later on in texturing.

❮ ❯
❮ ❯

Hair

Building the hair with curves and paths, since the design is structured as standing strands of thick hair. I've realized a better way of executing this is to make each "strand" thick so that it covers more area, and makes rendering later on much easier to handle.

UV Unwrap and Texturing

UV unwrap at the seams and aiming to have everything to be ordered in their regional pieces. The file will then be imported into Adobe Substance Painter to be textured. After texturing, the file will be brought back to Blender to connect texture nodes.

❮ ❯

Rigged and Animated

Weight painting on Blender was unrecorded. The most difficult parts were the hips, surprisingly, since the automatic weight painting applied was including various vertices that the weight painted parts had no associations with, specifically the parts where the wings and tail were. Professor told me that Blender isn't the wisest when it comes to weight painting. I am interested in attempting weight painting in Maya now.

Rigging to a running sequence was relatively easy to make.
